Week 1: Auspiciousness

Tuesday: Day One

Let me tell you about some of these words.

So it’s the first day of English. I’m late because of the long journey from gym. Apparently we have assigned seats. Ms. Clark made us move around excessively to get each of us in the right spot. She gave some introductory remarks and handed out the vocab sheets. We each “pick a word” so that we end up with 25 words on which we will be quizzed. I picked aghast. There’s a bit of time before lunch 2 [1], so she told us about some of those words. She begins to read off the definitions again. It’s really time for lunch, and eventually we go. When we get back, she tells us about Romans, and we watch Gladiator. #vocab #movie

Wednesday: Snow Day

It was a snow day. Not to use a vocab word, but this is quite auspicious.

Thursday: Day Two

So it’s the second day of English. Ms. Clark has to go somewhere else, or something. There’s a young sub who seems as amused as us that we will be spending the whole class coloring Roman and British soldiers. #sub #art

Friday: Day Three

The first part of class was spent messing around, although we could have been studying, reading, or coloring. After lunch we took our vocab quiz. Then we went to the Library to find ten Roman ruins in France and Africa. For others in the class, this assignment was different. We were supposed to read 45 pages of Grendyl, but we didn’t. #vocab #library

Notes

[1] Owing to the unique way in which our school day was scheduled, “lunch 2” occurred during the middle of the class.
